hypertext

HTML is an abbreviation for Hypertext Markup Language. Summarizing in one sentence: HTML is a language for publishing content (text, image, video, audio, etc.) on the Web.

HTML is based on the concept of Hypertext. Hypertext are sets of elements – or nodes – linked by links. These elements can be words, images, videos, audios, documents etc.

These elements together form a large information network.


They are not linked linearly as if they were texts in a book, where one subject is linked to the other over and over again.

The connection made in a hypertext is something unforeseen that allows the communication of data, organizing knowledge and storing related information.


To distribute information globally, there needs to be a language that is universally translated by various means of access. HTML presents itself as this language.

Developed by Tim Berners-Lee when HTML gained popularity Mosaic - browser developed by Marc Andreessen in 1990 - has gained traction.
